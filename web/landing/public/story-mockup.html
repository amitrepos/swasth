3am. Your phone rings. A call from home.

"We're taking Maa to hospital — chest pain."

By the time you land, she's in ICU. Heart attack. She'll survive — barely.

She stood by you through every thick and thin of your life.

And on the one night she needed you the most,
thousands of kilometres stood between you.

As soon as you walk in, the doctor turns to you:

"Is your mother diabetic? I'm surprised to see her cholesterol is high — and her BP too. Was anyone monitoring her, or did this happen suddenly?"
