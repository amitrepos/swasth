## PART 1: PRESS RELEASE

### Swasth Launches the World's First Integrated Device-and-AI Health Ecosystem That Helps People with Diabetes and Hypertension Live Measurably Longer

*Swasth connects blood glucose, blood pressure, heart rate, activity, sleep, nutrition, and medication into one AI-powered health dashboard that tells users exactly what to do each day to add years to their life — and lets their family watch over them from anywhere in the world.*

**Bengaluru, India — \[Launch Date\]** — Swasth today announced the launch of the world's first fully integrated health ecosystem designed to help people with chronic conditions like diabetes and hypertension live measurably longer, healthier lives. The Swasth system combines three Bluetooth-connected devices — a blood glucose monitor, a blood pressure monitor, and a smart health band — that continuously feed data into a single AI-powered dashboard. Unlike standalone devices that display isolated numbers with no context, Swasth sees the complete picture of a user's body and translates it into specific, doctor-reviewed, daily action plans. The system is available at [www.swasth.health](http://www.swasth.health) starting \[Launch Date\], with Hindi and English support at launch.

Today, more than 1.5 billion people live with diabetes and hypertension. A person with uncontrolled diabetes loses six to eight years of life expectancy. A person with unmanaged hypertension loses five. When both coexist — which they do in over sixty percent of cases — the risk of heart attack, stroke, kidney failure, and premature death compounds dramatically. Yet the science is clear: reducing HbA1c by just one percentage point lowers diabetes-related death by twenty-one percent. Managing blood pressure actively reduces cardiovascular death by forty-three percent. The World Health Organization confirms that eighty percent of premature heart attacks and strokes are entirely preventable. The medical knowledge to save these lives exists. What has been missing is a system that puts it into daily action.

Swasth solves this by connecting three devices through one AI engine built on clinical protocols and reviewed by licensed physicians. Every glucose reading is understood in the context of that day's sleep, physical activity, heart rate, food intake, and medication timing. The AI does not show a number — it explains why the number is what it is and tells the user exactly what to do today to make it better tomorrow. Over days and weeks, this creates a feedback loop that has never existed for ordinary patients: act, measure, learn, improve, live longer.

A photo-based food intelligence system identifies over 500 South Asian dishes and tells users how each meal will affect their body that day. A family monitoring feature lets up to three family members view a parent's full health dashboard in real time from anywhere in the world. An automatic monthly health report is delivered to the user's doctor, who can adjust medication through the app and have it delivered to the patient's door through Swasth's integrated pharmacy network. ## PART 2: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

### External FAQ (Customer)

**Q: How does Swasth actually help me live longer?** A: Published research shows that reducing HbA1c by one to two percentage points, lowering blood pressure by ten to fifteen mmHg, and adding consistent daily walking can extend life expectancy by years — potentially a decade. Swasth is engineered to drive exactly these outcomes. It connects all your health data into one picture and converts it into a specific daily action plan built on clinical protocols. The daily feedback loop — act, measure, learn, improve — produces compounding health gains. Rajesh Kumar, 58, in Lucknow saw his fasting sugar drop from 162 to 131 in one month. His doctor reviewed his Swasth report and reduced one of his medications. Sustained over years, those improvements translate directly into longer life.

**Q: What comes in the kit and how long does setup take?** A: Three Bluetooth devices (blood glucose monitor, upper-arm blood pressure monitor, smart health band tracking heart rate, SpO2, steps, and sleep), a starter pack of twenty-five test strips and lancets, charging cables, and a QR code guide. Setup takes under five minutes — download the app, scan the QR code, and all three devices pair automatically. It can be done remotely over a video call.

**Q: My parents are not comfortable with technology. Can they use this?** A: The product was designed so the person least comfortable with technology does the least. The health band is fully automatic — once worn, it captures everything without interaction. BP and glucose checks work identically to standalone devices; the only difference is readings sync to the app automatically. The app uses large fonts, simple icons, and voice interaction in Hindi and English. Total daily effort: under three minutes. Setup, configuration, and monitoring are handled by the adult child. If a user stops taking readings, the system alerts family members.

**Q: How does family monitoring work from abroad?** A: Purchase the kit online with international payment, ship it to your parents, set it up over video call. Download the Swasth Family app — you immediately have real-time visibility into every health metric. Configure custom alerts for any threshold. Up to three family members can monitor one user, coordinate care, and confirm medications have been delivered. Priya Menon in Singapore and Kavitha Iyer in London both set up their parents' kits over video call in under ten minutes.

**Q: How does the food photo feature work with Indian food?** A: Photograph your plate before eating. Swasth identifies the dish — dal chawal, dosa with sambar, biryani, idli, poha, or any of 500+ South Asian dishes — and estimates carbohydrate, sodium, protein, and calorie content. It then cross-references this against your current glucose trend, blood pressure, activity level, and medication timing to tell you how this specific meal will affect your body today. If photo recognition is uncertain, type what you are eating in natural language. The database expands to Southeast Asian and African cuisines before each market entry.

**Q: What are the subscription tiers?** A: Free: device syncing, unified dashboard, basic trends. Health+: AI daily coaching, photo food intelligence, medication management and reminders, Health Vault. Family Care: everything in Health+ plus real-time family monitoring (up to three members), monthly doctor reports via the Swasth Doctor Portal, and integrated pharmacy delivery. Annual plans at a discount.
