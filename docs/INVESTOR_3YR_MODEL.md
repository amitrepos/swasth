## 4. Investor math

### The ask
- **Raise: Rs 1.2cr** (covers Rs 83L cumulative loss + Rs 30L buffer = ~3-yr runway).
- **At Rs 6cr pre-money valuation** → investor takes 20% (post-money Rs 7.2cr).

### Expected return (no further dilution)

| Year | ARR | Valuation (10× ARR) | Investor 20% stake | Return on Rs 1.2cr |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Y1 end | 24L | Rs 2.4cr | Rs 48L | 0.4× (paper loss) |
| Y2 end | 1.08cr | Rs 11cr | Rs 2.2cr | 1.8× |
| Y3 end | 2.16cr | **Rs 21.6cr** | **Rs 4.3cr** | **3.6×** |

### Expected return with Series A dilution (realistic)
- Series A at Y2 end likely raises Rs 5-10cr at Rs 25-40cr post-money.
- Investor's 20% dilutes to ~12-14% post-A.
- Y3 stake @ 13% × Rs 21.6cr = Rs 2.8cr → **2.3× cash return**.
- Real upside: stake compounds to Series B and beyond.

### Indian health-tech reality check
- 10× ARR is optimistic; Indian early health-tech often gets 5-8×.
- @ 7× ARR, Y3 valuation Rs 15cr → 20% stake = Rs 3cr = 2.5×.
- @ 5× ARR, Rs 11cr → Rs 2.2cr = 1.8× (still positive).

## 5. City-level economics (the unit)

A "mature city" = 24 doctors × 10 paying = 240 paying patients.

### Revenue per mature city
- 240 × Rs 1,000/mo = **Rs 2.4L MRR / Rs 28.8L ARR per city**.

### Cost per mature city
| Head | Rs/mo |
|---|---:|
| 2 ops people | 50K |
| Doctor payouts (240 × Rs 500) | 1.2L |
| Local incidentals | 10K |
| **Total per city** | **1.8L/mo = Rs 21.6L/yr** |

### Per-city contribution margin (mature)
- Revenue 2.4L − City cost 1.8L = **Rs 60K/mo contribution per mature city**.
- 8 mature cities = Rs 4.8L/mo contribution → covers HQ (founder + dev + infra + ads ~ Rs 4L/mo) → **break-even at scale**.

## 6. Break-even and payback

- **Monthly break-even:** when 8 cities are mature (~Y3 mid).
- **Cumulative cash break-even:** ~Y4 (recovers the Rs 83L cumulative loss in ~12 months at scale).
- **Investor payback (cash return):** never via dividends. Exit only — Series A markup or acquisition.
- **Honest framing for investor:** "You don't get your money back; you exit at 3-5× via Series A markup or acquisition by Year 3-4."

## 7. Sensitivity: what kills the model

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| M3 retention < 40% | -50% ARR by Y3; raise needs to grow | Validate FOFO + coach playbook in Bangalore Y1 BEFORE city #2 |
| Doctors don't refer 10 paying patients | -40% ARR | Doctor onboarding playbook; track per-doctor patient count weekly |
| NRI willingness < 30% | -60% revenue (patients can't pay) | Razorpay test page validation pre-launch |
| Patna-style doctor revolt | Cost of finding replacement | Diversify across 8 doctors per city (not concentrated) |
| Series A doesn't happen | Have to operate from cash flow | Conservative cumulative loss ≤Rs 1cr keeps this option alive |
