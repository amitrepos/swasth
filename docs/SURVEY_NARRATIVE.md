# Swasth — Survey Narrative (NRI Audience)

> **Purpose:** 2-min read story used at the top of NRI surveys, waitlist landing pages, ad copy, and Facebook diaspora groups. Hook → problem → "what if" → solution → cost kick.
> **Audience:** NRI children of elderly Indian parents (UK, Germany, US, Gulf, Singapore, Canada).
> **Goal:** wake up the latent fear, position Swasth as the answer, drop the price as a soft kick at the end. Then ask survey questions / waitlist signup.
> **Locked:** 2026-05-02. Refined live with founder. Companion to `MOTHERBOOK.md`.

## The story (English — primary)

---

**The 3am call you don't want.**

3am. Your phone rings. A call from home. *"Maa ko hospital le ja rahe hain — chest pain."*

By the time you land, she's in ICU. Heart attack. She'll survive — barely.

Three days in ICU. Tests. Angioplasty. Flight back. Unpaid leave. **Rs 7 lakh in nine days.**

The money isn't the worst part. The worst part is sitting outside the ICU at 2am, scrolling her WhatsApp, wondering when her health started to drift. *You weren't watching. She was alone.*

**What if you could prevent this?**

Swasth is the daily health companion her doctor recommends. She tracks her medical metrics, her food, her physical activity — all through Swasth. Her doctor reviews weekly. The Swasth coaching team checks in. The AI quietly notices the drift no one else would catch.

Three sets of eyes. Every day. The slow rise in week 4 becomes a doctor's call in week 5 — not an ambulance in week 52.

**Imagine she has people who watch over her — the way she once watched over you.** Every quarter a report lands in your inbox: *"BP was trending up. Medication was adjusted. Cardiac risk: stable."* You took proactive action — and we didn't even have to tell you. You'll be surprised.

You sleep at night. She isn't alone. Neither are you.

**Rs 999 a month. Less than one weekly coffee. That's it.**

## Notes for use

- **Length:** ~230 words / ~2 min read.
- **Hook:** "The 3am call you don't want." — concrete, universal NRI fear.
- **Problem stage:** ICU + costs (Rs 7L, accurate range Rs 5-10L for cardiac event + travel + lost work).
- **Emotional twist:** "You weren't watching. She was alone." — the real wound, not money.
- **Solution stage:** Swasth as daily companion her doctor recommends. 360 capture (metrics + food + activity). Three watchers (doctor + coach + AI).
- **Reward stage:** quarterly proactive action without nagging. "You took proactive action — we didn't even have to tell you."
- **Reciprocity reframe:** "the way she once watched over you" — Indian filial-care anchor.
- **Cost kick at end:** Rs 999 framed against weekly coffee. Soft, not the focus.
