# Doctor Pitch Deck — Feedback & Action Items

## What Worked Well
- 30-day trend charts — "changes how I practice"
- Hindi interface + "under 3 minutes daily" — critical for elderly/rural patients
- Family dashboard — "if family can see readings from another city, that is powerful"
- Family involvement stat (adherence 34% → 69.5%) — compelling if real
- Compliance section (DPDPA, NMC, zero fee-splitting) — builds trust

## Gaps Identified

### P0 — Must address before doctor conversations

1. **"What do I actually DO during the 90 days?"**
   - Deck never tells the doctor their weekly time commitment
   - Fix: Prepare a 1-page "Doctor's Role" document
   - Suggested framing: "30 minutes in week 1 enrolling patients. After that, 10 min/week reviewing the monthly report we send you. That's it."

2. **No doctor on the founding team**
   - "Three Amazon tech people built a health app. I have seen this before."
   - Fix: Get at least one credible Indian doctor (endocrinologist or cardiologist) on advisory board ASAP — even before the pilot starts
   - Even a relative doctor who reviews the AI logic counts

3. **Stock options are meaningless to doctors**
   - "I don't understand stock options. This is vague on purpose."
   - Fix: In verbal conversation, translate to simple terms. Or consider a small honorarium (Rs 5-10K/month) to signal seriousness
   - Note: Per NMC rules, compensation must NOT be tied to number of patients referred (flat grant only)

4. **Liability question**
   - "If the AI gives a wrong action plan and patient follows it, who is liable?"
   - Fix: Prepare a clear answer. Current position: AI provides lifestyle coaching only, not diagnosis. App has disclaimers. Doctor is not liable for AI recommendations.
   - Get this reviewed by a startup lawyer

### P1 — Should address for stronger pitch

5. **"Clinical validation study" — what study?**
   - Doctors want to know: Is there ethics committee approval? CTRI registration?
   - If you put their name on something not methodologically sound, it hurts their reputation
   - Fix: Either get a basic ethics committee approval or be honest: "This is a product pilot, not a clinical trial. We will jointly publish the outcomes."

6. **Patient dropout plan**
   - "15 of 20 patients will stop by week 3"
   - Fix: Highlight existing engagement features (streaks, family leaderboard, gamification points)
   - Consider: WhatsApp reminders to patients who haven't logged in 3 days

7. **Offline capability**
   - "This is a Bihar pilot — internet is not reliable in Jehanabad, Nalanda"
   - Fix: This is already in the task tracker (A9 — offline mode). Prioritize for Bihar pilot.

8. **Who pays for extra test strips?**
   - Patients spend Rs 25-30 per strip. More frequent testing = more cost.
   - Fix: Decide policy — does Swasth provide starter strips? Or is existing frequency enough?

9. **Doctor portal / dashboard**
   - Deck shows patient-facing screenshots but nothing about what the doctor sees
   - Fix: Build a simple doctor view (even a web page with patient list + trends) before pilot

### P2 — Nice to have

10. **WhatsApp group with pilot doctors**
    - Doctors want to know they're not alone. Create a group for founding physicians.

11. **Reference from any other doctor**
    - Even a beta tester or relative who has seen the app. One testimonial.

12. **Local language beyond Hindi**
    - Bhojpuri, Magahi for voice/chatbot in Bihar context (future consideration)

## What the Deck Already Handles (but doctor may miss)

- **AI disclaimer**: App has "Not a medical diagnosis. Consult your doctor for clinical advice. All AI insights are for informational purposes" on the dashboard. AI chat also recommends talking to doctors.
  - Issue: Slide 4 mentions "AI Support not Diagnosis" briefly but a scanning doctor might miss it
  - Fix (verbal): Lead with "The AI never diagnoses — it only coaches lifestyle and always directs back to you"

- **NMC fee-splitting**: Called out on Slide 4 as "zero fee-splitting" — good

- **Data security**: DPDPA, encryption, consent — all covered on Slide 4

## What Would Get a Doctor to Say Yes

1. 15-minute live demo in their clinic with a real device (Accu-Chek, Dr. Morepen)
2. One credible Indian doctor already on advisory board
3. Simple 1-page Advisor Agreement (template already drafted — see lawyer consultation)
4. Small honorarium or at minimum clear explanation of stock option value
5. Ethics committee approval or honest framing of what the pilot is
6. WhatsApp group with other pilot doctors
