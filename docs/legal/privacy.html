Swasth — Privacy Policy

App: Swasth (health.swasth.app)
Effective date: 17 April 2026
Last updated: 11 June 2026

Swasth ("we", "us") is a personal health monitoring application for patients in India. This policy explains what data we collect, why, how it's used, and your rights under India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (DPDP) and the Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules 2011 (SPDI).

1. Data We Collect

Swasth collects: blood glucose readings, blood pressure readings, pulse rate, meal notes, profile information (name, age, gender, medical conditions, medications), and photos of medical devices for automated reading capture.

2. Purpose of Collection

Your health data is used to: display trends and health scores, generate personalised health insights, share with your designated family members, and provide information to your doctor for treatment.

3. AI Processing

Swasth uses third-party AI services — Google Gemini and DeepSeek — to generate health recommendations. A summarised version of your data (averages and ranges, not individual readings) is sent to these services. You can opt out of AI processing at any time; rule-based insights will be used instead.

4. Data Sharing

Your data is shared only with: family members you explicitly invite, AI services (Google Gemini, DeepSeek) for health insights if you consent, and your doctor if you choose to share. We do not sell or share your data with advertisers or any other third parties.

5. Doctor Data Sharing

If you choose to link a doctor to your profile, Swasth shares your health data with that specific doctor so they can monitor your condition and provide clinical guidance. Linking happens only when you (or a family member authorised to act on your behalf) explicitly enter the doctor's code and complete the in-app consent screen.

What is shared with a linked doctor: your health readings (blood glucose, blood pressure, pulse, SpO2), health trends and scores, and profile information (name, age, gender, medical conditions, medications). What is NOT shared: your AI Doctor chat history and the data of any other family members on your account.

Legal basis (DPDPA 2023, Section 6): we share this data only on your explicit, informed consent, for the specific purpose of health monitoring and clinical guidance. This is a sensitive-personal-data sharing governed by the purpose-limitation and data-minimisation principles — your data is shared only with the doctor you linked and only for that purpose.

Your control and revocation: you can see every doctor currently linked to your profile, and you can revoke a doctor's access at any time from Settings → Connected Doctors → Stop Sharing. Revocation takes effect immediately; the doctor can no longer view your data. Doctors can never modify or delete your readings. Every time a doctor views your data or adds a note, it is recorded in an audit log.

6. Clinical Notes

A doctor linked to your profile may record clinical notes about you — for example, observations on a reading or guidance on your condition. These notes are written by the doctor, encrypted at rest, and are part of the medical record the doctor is professionally required to keep.

Your access to clinical notes: a clinical note is visible to you in the app only when the doctor explicitly chooses to share that note with you. Notes the doctor keeps private are not displayed in your app. Under Indian law you nonetheless have the right to request a copy of medical records pertaining to you; to do so, contact the doctor or our Grievance Officer (see Contact below).

Retention: clinical notes are medical records. When you delete your Swasth account, your personal health data is permanently removed; however, a doctor's clinical notes may be retained as anonymised medical records (with your name, contact details and profile identifier removed) for the period required by medical-record-retention rules, to comply with the doctor's professional obligations. This is an exception to general deletion driven by sectoral medical regulation.

Clinical notes are clinical observations, not prescriptions, and do not by themselves constitute a diagnosis or a directive to change any medication. Always consult your doctor before acting on them.

7. WhatsApp Messaging

With your consent, Swasth sends certain health notifications to you (and to family members you have authorised as emergency contacts) over WhatsApp, using the Twilio WhatsApp Business API (a third-party messaging provider). These messages may include: immediate alerts when a critical reading is recorded, and optional weekly health summaries.

What data leaves the platform: a WhatsApp message may contain the patient's name, the reading type and value, a severity indication, and a call to action. This information is transmitted through Twilio and Meta (WhatsApp) infrastructure to reach your phone. We send the minimum information needed for the recipient to understand and act on the alert.

Legal basis (DPDPA 2023, Section 6): WhatsApp notifications are sent on your consent for the specific purpose of timely health monitoring and safety alerts. We do not use WhatsApp for marketing and we do not share your number with advertisers.

Opt-out: you can turn off WhatsApp messages at any time via Settings → Notifications in the app, or by contacting us at swasth.admin@gmail.com. Message delivery is also subject to WhatsApp's and Twilio's own terms and privacy practices.

8. Security Measures

We protect your data with: AES-256 encryption for health readings and doctor clinical notes stored in our database, bcrypt password hashing, JWT-based authentication with token expiration, TLS/HTTPS for all data in transit, and encrypted local storage on your device.

9. Data Retention

Your data is stored as long as your account is active. You may request deletion of all your data at any time through the app settings. Upon account deletion, all readings, profiles, AI logs, and personal information are permanently removed. Note the exception in Section 6 (Clinical Notes): a doctor's clinical notes may be retained in anonymised form as medical records where required by medical-record-retention rules.

10. Your Rights

Under Indian data protection law (SPDI Rules 2011 and DPDP Act 2023), you have the right to: access your data, correct inaccuracies, withdraw consent, request deletion of your data, and file a grievance. To exercise these rights, use the in-app settings or contact us at the address below.

11. Children's Privacy

Swasth is intended for adult users (18+). We do not knowingly collect data from children under 18. If we learn we have collected data from a minor without parental consent, we will delete it.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy. Material changes will be communicated in-app. The "Last updated" date above reflects the current version.
